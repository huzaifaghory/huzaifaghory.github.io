Acoustic Bubble Detection
in Fluid Domains

A numerical model for detecting micro-bubbles in fluid media using ultrasonic piezoelectric transducers — correlating bubble scattering cross-section with resonance frequency to identify optimal detection bands.

0–2 µm bubble range
FSI Fluid-structure interaction
Minnaert Resonance validated
Fig. 1 — Harmonic response analysis showing pressure wave propagation through the fluid domain
Objective

To develop a numerical model for detecting micro-bubbles in a fluid medium using ultrasonic piezoelectric transducers — correlating the scattering cross-section of bubbles with specific observation frequencies.

Detecting small bubbles in hydraulic or medical systems is critical but difficult due to noise and signal attenuation. The core challenge was accurately modelling the fluid-solid interaction between the piezoelectric sensor and the water domain, to predict how bubbles of varying sizes scatter acoustic waves.

Technical Details
Simulation Platform ANSYS — Coupled Field Harmonic Response
Signal Processing MATLAB Signal Processing Toolbox
Physics Fluid-Structure Interaction (FSI)
Sensor Material PZT 5A— Lead Zirconate Titanate
Domain Geometry 2D axisymmetric
Bubble Size Range 0 µm – 2000 µm radius
Key Variable Bubble resonance vs. transducer frequency
Output Metric Target strength & scattering cross-section
Methodology
1
Transducer Modelling
Modelled the PZT sensor in ANSYS, defining piezoelectric material properties to accurately simulate the conversion of electrical voltage into mechanical vibration and radiated acoustic pressure.
2
Harmonic Frequency Sweep
Conducted a frequency sweep across the operating range to identify the resonance frequencies of bubbles from 0 µm to 2000 µm radius — mapping the Minnaert resonance relationship between bubble size and peak scattering frequency.
3
Coupled Field Simulation
Set up a 2D axisymmetric domain in ANSYS to simulate ultrasonic wave propagation and reflection off the bubble surface. The coupled field formulation captured piezoelectric, structural, and acoustic physics simultaneously.
4
Signal Processing in MATLAB
Exported acoustic pressure data to MATLAB to calculate target strength and scattering cross-section across the frequency range — producing characteristic curves for each bubble size.
Fig. 2 — MATLAB plot of scattering cross-section vs. frequency for bubble radii of 0–2 mm
Results
Minnaert validated
Clear correlation established between bubble radius and peak scattering frequency — confirming the Minnaert resonance model and validating the numerical approach against theoretical predictions.
Frequency bands
kHz range effective for larger bubbles; MHz required for micro-bubbles — providing a practical frequency selection guide for acoustic detection systems in hydraulic and medical applications.
Cavitation detection
Optimised frequency band proposed for early detection of cavitation bubbles in hydraulic pumps — enabling preventive monitoring before physical damage occurs.
