Objective

To design a two-stage spur gear reduction unit to specific input power and speed requirements, strictly adhering to ISO 6336 standards for gear capacity and DIN 743 for shaft fatigue design.

The core challenge was balancing compactness against safety margin. A smaller gearbox runs hotter and risks tooth failure — requiring iterative calculation of gear module and face width to withstand both pitting (surface fatigue) and bending stress simultaneously, without over-engineering the housing.

Technical Details
Gear Standard ISO 6336 — spur gear capacity
Shaft Standard DIN 743 — shaft fatigue analysis
Calculation Tool MATLAB — iterative optimisation scripts
CAD & Assembly SolidWorks
Gear Material Case-hardened steel 18CrNiMo7-6
Bearings SKF deep groove ball bearings
Bearing Life Target 10,000 hours
Components Spur gears, shafts, keyways, housing
Methodology
1
Material Selection
Selected case-hardened steel 18CrNiMo7-6 for its high surface hardness and fatigue resistance — essential for achieving the required safety factors within a compact envelope at the target operating loads.
2
MATLAB Gear Optimisation
Wrote a MATLAB script automating iterative application of the Lewis Bending Equation and Hertzian Contact Stress formulas — enabling rapid optimisation of gear module (m) and face width across a full parameter sweep without manual recalculation.
3
Shaft Design
Computed shear, bending, and torsion diagrams for each shaft to determine minimum diameters at critical cross-sections — bearing seats, gear seats, and keyway steps — using DIN 743 fatigue strength methodology.
4
Bearing Selection & Full Documentation
Selected SKF deep groove ball bearings based on calculated radial and axial loads for 10,000-hour service life. Produced a complete drawing set: Bill of Materials, section views, and GD&T tolerancing for all mating fits.
Results
SF 1.2 / 1.5
Contact and bending safety factors both achieved — 1.2× for pitting resistance and 1.5× for tooth bending, meeting ISO 6336 minimums with margin across both gear stages.
−12%
Housing volume reduced vs. initial baseline — achieved through MATLAB-driven module optimisation finding a more compact gear geometry without sacrificing the required safety margin.
