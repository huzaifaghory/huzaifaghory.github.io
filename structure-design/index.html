Project 04 FEA SolidWorks Fabrication

Design & Manufacturing of a
Load Bearing Structure

End-to-end design, fabrication and physical load testing of a minimal-material aluminium truss structure — using topology optimisation and FEA to eliminate dead weight while maintaining structural integrity under dynamic load.

200% Of design load held
−15% Weight vs. baseline
−25% Mass via cutouts
Fig. 1 — The final fabricated structure undergoing physical stress testing
Objective

To design and fabricate a load-bearing structure capable of supporting a specific weight while minimising the structure's own mass — adhering to strict material constraints and geometric limitations.

The primary constraint was the strength-to-weight ratio. With a limited material budget, the structure had to resist dynamic loads without buckling. The key insight was identifying zero-force members in the truss and eliminating them — saving weight without compromising integrity.

Technical Details
CAD Software SolidWorks, Siemens NX 11
FEA Tool SolidWorks Simulation
Material Aluminium Sheet — Al 6061
Optimisation Method Topology optimisation for weight reduction
Fabrication Laser cutting, bending, manual assembly
File Output DXF files for laser cutter
Truss Configurations Warren vs. Pratt — comparative analysis
Failure Mode Buckling (predicted and confirmed by FEA)
Methodology
1
Conceptual Design
Sketched and evaluated multiple truss configurations — Warren vs. Pratt truss geometries — analysing member force distributions analytically before committing to a geometry for simulation.
2
FEA Stress Analysis
Used SolidWorks Simulation to identify high-stress concentration areas and potential buckling points under the design load. Stress contours guided material redistribution decisions.
3
Topology Optimisation & Refinement
Removed material from low-stress regions by introducing strategic cutouts, reducing total mass by 25% while keeping peak stresses below the yield threshold. Zero-force members were eliminated entirely.
4
Fabrication & Assembly
Generated DXF files for laser cutting from Al 6061 sheet stock. Parts were bent to geometry and assembled using an interlocking joint system, avoiding welding for repeatability and precision.
Results
200%
Load capacity exceeded — the structure successfully held twice the design load before failure, demonstrating a substantial safety margin above specification.
−15%
Final weight below baseline — the optimised design was 15% lighter than the initial configuration, achieved entirely through FEA-guided material removal.
FEA confirmed
Predicted buckling mode matched physical failure exactly — validating the simulation model and confirming the accuracy of the boundary conditions and material parameters used.
