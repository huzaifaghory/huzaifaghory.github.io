Eddy Current Testing
Probe Design

Design, fabrication, and testing of a custom ECT probe built from scratch — capable of detecting surface flaws in conductive materials and distinguishing defect signals from liftoff noise.

2-3 kHz operating range
AWG 32 Copper coil wire
Low-cost vs. commercial probe
Fig. 1 — 3D render of the custom coil probe assembly designed in Fusion 360
Objective

To design, fabricate, and test a custom Eddy Current Testing probe capable of detecting surface flaws in conductive materials — demonstrating the principles of electromagnetic induction and signal impedance analysis.

Commercial ECT probes are expensive, closed systems. The challenge was to build a low-cost, functional probe from scratch that could distinguish between a crack (a real defect) and liftoff (distance variation from the surface) — a common and difficult source of noise in NDT.

Technical Details
EM Simulation FEMM (Finite Element Method Magnetics)
CAD Design Autodesk Fusion 360 (probe casing)
Signal Analysis MATLAB — impedance plotting & processing
Circuit Design Wheatstone Bridge configuration
Coil Hardware Copper coil AWG 32, ferrite core
Test Equipment Oscilloscope, function generator
Operating Frequency 2 kHz – 3 kHz
Test Material WAAM Sample with manufactured discontinuities
Methodology
1
Coil Design
Calculated the optimal number of turns and wire gauge to achieve the target inductance for the operating frequency range of 2–3 kHz. Coil geometry balanced sensitivity against spatial resolution.
2
Circuit Design — Wheatstone Bridge
Implemented a Wheatstone Bridge circuit to null the probe baseline and maximise sensitivity to small impedance changes caused by subsurface or surface defects.
3
FEMM Simulation
Used FEMM to simulate magnetic field density distribution and predict how induced eddy currents would interact with a theoretical surface crack — validating coil geometry before fabrication.
4
Physical Testing & Calibration
Created calibration blocks with artificial defects (slits of varying depth) to verify probe sensitivity. Mapped the impedance plane to characterise defect vs. liftoff signal separation.
Fig. 2 — FEMM contour plot showing eddy current penetration depth and magnetic flux distribution
Results
Defect detected
Surface flaws successfully identified on an aluminium test block. The probe reliably detected artificial slit defects across the calibrated frequency range.
Liftoff mapped
Impedance plane curve characterised — demonstrating clear phase separation between liftoff signals and genuine defect signals, a key challenge in ECT practice.
Edge effect
Signal distortion near workpiece edges characterised — establishing a measurable dead zone limit and informing practical probe positioning guidelines.
