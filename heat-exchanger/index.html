Conjugate Heat Transfer
Analysis of a Heat Exchanger

Optimisation of a shell-and-tube heat exchanger fluid domain to maximise heat transfer efficiency while minimising pressure drop — achieved through modified baffle geometry and parametric CFD simulation.

+15% Heat transfer coefficient
<5% Validation deviation
1.9M Mesh cells
Fig. 1 — Velocity streamlines showing fluid flow through the exchanger
Objective

To analyse the fluid domain of a heat exchanger and optimise design parameters to maximise heat transfer efficiency while minimising pressure drop.

The initial design suffered from uneven flow distribution, creating dead zones where heat transfer was negligible. The core challenge was to modify the baffle geometry to induce better mixing without significantly increasing pumping power.

Technical Details
CFD Platform SimScale (cloud-based)
Post-Processing MATLAB
Fluid Model Turbulent Flow — k-epsilon
Simulation Type Steady-state conjugate heat transfer
Mesh Type Hex-dominant, 1.9M cells
Boundary Layer y⁺ < 1 (near-wall refinement)
CAD Tool Fusion 360
Mesh Verification Grid independence study conducted
Methodology
1
Geometry Preparation
Cleaned CAD model in Fusion 360 to ensure watertight geometry suitable for CFD meshing. Removed small features that would cause mesh quality issues.
2
Meshing & Grid Independence
Conducted a mesh independence study across three refinement levels. Refined the mesh near boundary layers with y⁺ < 1 to accurately capture near-wall turbulence. Final mesh: 1.9 million tetrahedral elements.
3
CFD Simulation
Ran steady-state simulations in SimScale using the k-epsilon turbulence model. Parametric study varied baffle spacing and inlet velocity to map efficiency against pressure drop.
4
Validation
Compared pressure drop results against theoretical calculations based on Bernoulli's principle. Deviation of less than 5% confirmed simulation accuracy.
Fig. 2 — Temperature distribution contour with fine mesh (1.9M cells) used to conduct mesh independence study
Results
+15%
Increase in heat transfer coefficient achieved through modified baffle geometry that induced better flow mixing across the core elements.
Uniform
Flow distribution across all core elements — dead zones eliminated. Velocity streamlines confirmed even coverage throughout the fluid domain.
<5%
Deviation from theoretical pressure drop — validating the simulation setup against Bernoulli's principle calculations.
